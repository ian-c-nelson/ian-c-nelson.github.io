About Me

My name is Ian Nelson and I am a non-binary trans web developer! I have been programming since got my first computer, a Commodore 64. For the last 15 years I have been working in the .Net Framework environment primarily building Web Applications with C# on the back end and jQuery & jQuery-UI on the front end. Our data storage was done through both Microsoft SQL Server and Oracle RDBMS. We also used Web Api 2.0 and MVC 4.0.

Recently, I began attending University of Arizona Coding Bootcamp so that I could expand my skillset to include the MERN stack (MongoDB, Express, React, and Node.js). I really enjoy learning new (to me) Technologies, and I felt that it would also make me a more rounded and desirable web developer. I am looking forward to all of the new things I will be able to do at the end of the course!

As a non-binary trans-person, I don't really fit on either end of the gender spectrum. I have struggled with my gender all of my life. As a Gen-X kid in a small town, there was little understanding or acceptance of people who were different. The world has change a lot and I have finally started to find peace with who I am. I think this is important because it gives me another perspective on the world from cis-gendered people. I feel like it allows me to be more accepting of other people's differences and the perspectives that gives them. Whether it is informed by ethnicity, culture, gender, or sexual orientation. The world is a big beautiful place full of vibrant and unique people, and together we are greater than the sum of our parts.

All those moments will be lost in time, like tears in rain...
